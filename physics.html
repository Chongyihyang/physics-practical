Data collection

      - Take 2 readings to find an average
      - Take the smallest division of an instrument
      - For stop watch, read to one dp
      - For recorded readings, to find the average, keep to the same dp as readings
      - UNITS Check that they are in SI unit form
      - percentage uncertainty: 2sf
      - absolute uncertainty: 1sf
      - answer + absolute uncertainty: same dp as absolute uncertainty

Table

      - Label the graph with units
      - input the 2 values and the average values
      - For derived values, lowest sf of the input values or + 1 of it
      - For values, use pen!
      - Must have 6 data readings for line, 8 for curve

Graph

      - Use calculator to find the gradient and y-intercept
      - To find range: take
      (max value - min value) / number of lines
      OR
      (max value - regression value) / number of lines
      - When stating the value of gradient and y-intercept, units must be shown

Data analysis

    - Justify sf

      The justification should reflect students are clear of the rules for significant figures.
      For addition and subtraction, the answer should follow the lowest decimal place.
      For product and quotient, the answer should follow the lowest significant figure.

      “The significant figures for x follows the least number of significant figures in
      (A + B) and (C - D).”

    - Anomalous data

      If no anomalous data:

      “All the plotted points are close to and evenly distributed to the line
      of best fit, therefore there is no anomalous data.”

    - Determine if relationship is supported using two sets of data

      1. Calculate constant values from the table
      2. (k_max - k_min)/k_min * 100 %
      3. Find % uncertainty of all variables. Take the one with the largest % uncertainty (D)
      4.
      If percentage difference of k < percentage uncertainty in D:

      “Since the percentage difference of k is less than that of the percentage uncertainty in D, the
      results support the relationship.”


      If percentage difference of k > percentage uncertainty in D:

      “Since the percentage difference of k is more than that of the percentage uncertainty in D, the
      results does not support the relationship.”

Source of error and its improvements

      2021

    1) Unable to accurately judge the location to measure the diameter d of the water surface, hence the diameter could be larger or smaller than the true value.
    2) Measurement of diameter d using the venier calipers does not account for thickness of container wall, hence measurement is larger than the true value.
    3) Response time for thermometer is slower as it takes time for the alcohol to expand in the bulb. Hence, temperature recorded by thermometer θ does not reflect the actual temperature in the water.
    4) Unable to take simultaneous readings of temperature θ at the exact time of 2 minutes. The temperature could have changed as we switch from reading one instrument to another.
    5) The cup and bowl is made of paper and changes shape easily. Hence the diameter d of the water surface is not uniform during measurement.

    1) Use a transparent container so that the location can be more accurately judged.
    OR
    Use containers of constant cross-sectional surface area so that the diameter can be measured with the inner-jaws of the vernier caliper at the top of the container.
    2) Measure the thickness of the container with a micrometer screw gauge and subtract that from the diameter measurement.
    3) Use a temperature sensor connected to a datalogger to measure the temperature of water.
    4) Take a video of the setup when the reading is taken, ensuring the stopwatch and thermometer can both be seen in the video. Read off the temperature at the 2 minute mark from the video.
    5) Use a rigid material for the cup and bowl like styrofoam or plastic.
